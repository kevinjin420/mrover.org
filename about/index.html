The Mission

The Michigan Mars Rover Team designs, builds, and tests a Mars rover prototype to compete in the University Rover Challenge. Our interdisciplinary team of students pushes the boundaries of what's possible in student-led space exploration.

Team

Mechanical

01

Robotic Arm

Designs and builds a five degree-of-freedom robotic arm for Equipment Servicing and Extreme Retrieval missions.

02

Mobility

Develops drive and suspension systems to navigate rocks and rough terrain in each competition mission.

03

Chassis and Mounts

Develops a lightweight and strong chassis optimized for integration of all sub-systems.

Team

Science-Mechanical

01

Science Payload Instrumentation

Brings together sensors and tests for in-situ sampling with on-board life-detection tests.

02

Science Payload Acquisition

Develops and tests soil collection mechanics with a linear actuator-driven auger system.

Team

Science

01

Astrobiology

Develops tests to analyze soil and rock samples for signs of past or present life.

Team

Software

01

Autonomy

Handles Navigation, Perception, and Localization. Uses A* to path plan around obstacles.

02

Embedded Software

Writes low-level driver code for other programming subteams to utilize electronic equipment.

03

Teleoperation

Creates the interface between driver and rover, maintaining GUIs and control solutions.

04

Drone

Develops a drone for collaboration with the Rover, capable of manual and autonomous operation.

Team

Electrical

01

Power

Provides power to the rover and manages the electronics box with custom distribution solutions.

02

Embedded Hardware

Controls actuators, receives sensor signals, and designs custom PCBs for motor control.

03

Communications

Ensures strong wireless RF communication link between base station and rover.
